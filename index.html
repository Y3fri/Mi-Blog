PWA WEB

I worked on a project that consists of a PWA (Progressive Web Application) designed for users to download the app on their phones. The purpose of this application is to facilitate the collection of recyclable materials directly from users' homes, where they can exchange these materials for points that can later be redeemed for cleaning products.

The main objective of this project is to raise awareness about the importance of recycling without the need to leave their homes. Although the application is already developed, it is not yet in production.

This is a form to request the collection of recyclable materials. Users need to enter their personal information and location. A Google Maps API was also used to specify the exact pickup location, allowing the service to accurately locate the collection address.

The application also includes an administrative section that provides various tables for querying, creating, editing, and deleting data. Here, you can manage products for points redemption, register users (both workers and administrators), as well as schedule dates and times for material pickups and other related functions.

RNA BACKPROPAGATION

This is an artificial neural network (ANN) to identify fish. On the left, we have the trout fish and on the right, a more colorful fish, the Pomacanthus navarchus. The idea is to identify these fish.

First, I performed image processing on the photos of the fish, passing them through blue, red, or green channels. Then, I tried to remove the background and apply a kernel to make the images look as shown below. What we aim to do is extract the fish's contour and segment it for identification.

In the training part, we processed the image by reducing it to 70x70 pixels, which means 4900 inputs. The ANN has a hidden layer with 7 neurons and an output layer with 1 neuron. The outputs are coded as follows: if it is 0, it is the Pomacanthus navarchus fish, and if it is 1, it is the trout fish.

Here is the demonstration with other images of the fish. We can see that the neural network identifies them very well and approximates the desired outputs. In this case, we aim for 0, but it gives us 0.3618.

In this other case, as it is trout, it should approximate 1, but it gives us an output of 0.8631.
